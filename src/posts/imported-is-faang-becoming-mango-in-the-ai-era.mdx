<h2>
  
  
  Is FAANG Becoming MANGO in the AI Era?
</h2>

<p>For years, <strong>FAANG</strong> was the gold standard for innovation and engineering excellence.</p>

<p>If you were a developer, working at companies like Facebook (Meta), Apple, Amazon, Netflix, or Google was often seen as the ultimate career goal.</p>

<p>But the AI revolution is changing the conversation.</p>

<p>Today, some of the most influential companies aren't just building products—they're building intelligence. The spotlight is increasingly shifting toward AI-native organizations such as <strong>OpenAI</strong>, <strong>Anthropic</strong>, <strong>NVIDIA</strong>, and others that are shaping the future of software.</p>

<h2>
  
  
  The Bigger Shift
</h2>

<p>This isn't really about replacing FAANG with another acronym.</p>

<p>It's about a fundamental shift in technology:</p>

<ul>
<li>Search → Answers</li>
<li>Automation → Agents</li>
<li>Software → Intelligence</li>
<li>Features → Capabilities</li>
</ul>

<p>As developers, we're entering an era where understanding AI is becoming as important as understanding frameworks, databases, and system design.</p>

<h2>
  
  
  What This Means for Engineers
</h2>

<p>The most valuable engineers of the next decade will likely combine:</p>

<ul>
<li>Strong software engineering fundamentals</li>
<li>AI-assisted development skills</li>
<li>Prompt engineering</li>
<li>LLM and agent integration</li>
<li>AI-powered product thinking</li>
</ul>

<p>The goal isn't to compete with AI.</p>

<p>The goal is to learn how to build with it.</p>
