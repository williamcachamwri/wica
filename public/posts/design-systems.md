A design system should feel like a well-tuned engine — powerful but quiet.

## The role of whitespace

Whitespace is not empty space. It is visual rest. In the same way a musician
places rests between notes, a designer places space between elements to give
the eye room to breathe.

### Why rhythm matters

Good design has a pulse. Consistent spacing creates a beat that the user
subconsciously follows. When every card, button, and heading sits on the same
vertical rhythm, the interface becomes predictable — and predictability is a
form of trust.

## Intentional constraints

The best constraints are the ones you choose for yourself.

- A two-colour palette forces you to think in contrast, not colour.
- A single typeface makes hierarchy a typographic challenge, not a font
  catalogue.
- A 4px grid may seem restrictive, until you realise every alignment
  becomes deliberate.

## Building for change

A design system that is too rigid will crack under pressure. Leave room for
exceptions. Document why the rule exists, so when someone breaks it, they
know exactly what they are trading off.

Rules without principles are just opinions. Principles without rules are
wishful thinking.
